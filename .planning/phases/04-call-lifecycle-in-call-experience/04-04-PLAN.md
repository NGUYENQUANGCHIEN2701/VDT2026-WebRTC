## Phase Goal

**As a** caller or callee, **I want to** see a ringing incoming-call screen with the option to accept, reject, or cancel — and clear feedback when the other side is busy or doesn't answer — **so that** the call lifecycle feels like a real product rather than a fragile handshake.

<objective>
First frontend vertical slice: refactor the client from Phase 3's client-driven handshake to rendering the server's authoritative state, then deliver the ringing / accept / reject / cancel / busy / missed / glare experience end-to-end. After Plan 03 the backend emits CallStateChanged; this plan makes the client render it. After this plan a real user can ring another user, see the incoming screen with ringtone, accept or reject, cancel while ringing, get a busy toast, and get a missed toast.

Purpose: Per D-01, the client must become a render-state machine (intents in, state out). This is the largest frontend refactor of the phase and the foundation for the in-call and recovery slices.

Output: Extended callStore + refactored callActions/messages + IncomingCallCard ringtone + Toast component + ringtone asset. callStore.test.ts (Plan 01) goes GREEN.
</objective>
